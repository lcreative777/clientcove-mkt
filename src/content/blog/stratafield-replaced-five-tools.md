> "We replaced ClickUp, Notion, DocuSign, Dropbox, and Harvest with ClientCove. Our clients log into our domain, see our brand, and we cut $14,400 a year off our SaaS bill in the process."
>
> — **Sara Chen**, Founder, Stratafield Agency

## The setup

Stratafield is a 12-person creative agency in Austin running 38 active client engagements. Before ClientCove, their stack looked like this:

| Tool | What it did | Monthly cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ClickUp Business | Projects + tasks | $228 |
| Notion Plus | Docs + wiki | $120 |
| DocuSign Standard | E-signature | $45 |
| Dropbox Business | File sharing | $180 |
| Harvest | Time tracking | $144 |
| QuickBooks Online | Invoicing | $90 |
| Loom Business | Async video | $120 |
| Slack Pro | Team chat | $87 |
| **Total** | | **$1,014/mo** |

Plus a custom-built client login page that nobody loved.

## What changed

They moved everything except QuickBooks (accounting integration is sticky), Loom (video is video), and Slack (team chat is team chat) onto a single ClientCove install on `portal.stratafield.com`.

| Module | Replaces |
| --- | --- |
| Projects | ClickUp |
| Resources | Notion + Dropbox |
| Contracts | DocuSign |
| Tasks + Time | Harvest |
| Invoices | (Pushed to QuickBooks via Stripe) |

## The numbers

- **$1,014/mo → $0/mo** for the SaaS subscriptions they cut
- **$349** one-time for the Single Site license (year one updates included)
- **$149/yr** optional update plan after year one
- **Net first-year savings: $11,819**
- **Five-year savings (at flat 2026 prices): $60,343**

## What surprised them

> "The thing we didn't expect was how much our clients liked it. They went from logging into five different tools — or, more often, asking us to send links — to having one bookmark. Approval cycles dropped from days to hours. The portal lives at portal.stratafield.com. Our clients have never heard of ClientCove. That's exactly how we wanted it."

## What they wish they'd done sooner

- **Migrated DocuSign first.** The e-signature module was a one-day swap, immediate cost saving, and clients didn't care which signature provider they were tapping through.
- **Used the Resources module for finished assets.** They started with internal docs, but the real win was using it as the deliverable handoff library — clients no longer chase "which Dropbox link was the final?"

## What they're using next

The team is testing the new **time-tracking → invoice automation** in v3.2, which auto-generates Stripe invoices from approved timesheets. Next quarter they're moving payroll-adjacent reporting into a custom field setup on the time module.

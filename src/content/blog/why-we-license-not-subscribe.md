When we started building ClientCove, the SaaS playbook was already a decade old: charge per seat, per month, forever. Lock the data behind a login you don't own. Ratchet pricing every renewal cycle. The numbers worked — for the vendor.

For the businesses *using* the software, the math is uglier than it looks. A 12-person team paying $30/seat across five tools is $1,800 a month. Across five years, that's **$108,000** to rent a stack you can't take with you.

We did that math. Then we did it again from the other side.

## Pay once. Own forever.

ClientCove is sold as a perpetual license — $497 for a single site, $997 for an Agency License with source-code access. You pay once. You install on your VPS. Year one of updates is included. After that, annual updates are optional ($97/yr Single, $297/yr Agency).

If you skip a year of updates, your install keeps working. Forever. No license server to phone home to. No "trial expired" screen. No surprise bill when your team grows.

## The trade-off, honestly

Self-hosted software is a small lift up front. You need a $5/mo VPS or a managed plan. You need to run database backups (or let us do it). You need to update once a year if you want the new modules.

In exchange:

- **You own the data.** It's in your Postgres, on your server, behind your firewall.
- **You own the brand.** Your domain, your logo, your colors. Clients log into *your* portal — they never see ours.
- **You own the cost.** Adding 50 users doesn't cost you $1,500/mo more.
- **You own the future.** If we vanish tomorrow, your install runs. Forever.

## Who this isn't for

If you want zero infrastructure responsibility, the managed plan exists. If you want a per-seat product with a slick dashboard and 47 integrations, [there are excellent ones](https://copilot.com). We'll never compete with them on those axes.

ClientCove is for service businesses that have done the math, gotten tired of the per-seat tax, and want their portal under their control.

## What it costs us

Going perpetual-license instead of SaaS means our revenue isn't recurring by default. We can't show investors a hockey stick. We can't promise 18-month payback periods.

In return, we get customers who actually own the product, treat it like an asset, and tell other people about it for the right reasons. Five years in, that's been the better trade.
